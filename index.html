Short bio

Ingrid Røshol is from Hamar and lives in Oslo. She works at JACK & JONES (Storo). She likes buying vintage, enjoys music, and sings jazz.

In her sewing practice, she focuses on upcycling: working with garments and textiles that already exist, and letting the material constraints shape the design. For example, jeans can become tops, bedsheets can become purses, and “scrap” fabric becomes the starting point rather than the problem.

Alongside making, she researches digital waste and internet sustainability — especially the idea that deletion is often reclassification, not disappearance. She uses the upcycling mindset as a way to think about digital leftovers: what persists online, what is hidden in infrastructure, and what it means to “throw away” something digital.
